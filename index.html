Research

Dissertation — In Progress

Congressional Party Control and Federal Inspector General Budgets

Applies policy process theory to federal OIG budget outcomes, examining how political and institutional factors shape resource allocation for independent oversight. Uses a panel quantitative framework to analyze budget dynamics across the IG community.

Applied Research

Public Library Funding and Policy Attention: A PET Application

Extends Punctuated Equilibrium Theory to public library funding, examining whether attention dynamics in congressional and media venues predict punctuated budget changes across approximately 9,000 library systems using IMLS Public Library Survey panel data.
